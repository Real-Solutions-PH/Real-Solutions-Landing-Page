## 1. Company Overview

**Name (placeholder):** Real Solutions PH
**Location:** Philippines (remote-first, PH-based team)
**Founders:**

- UX Designer / Product Strategist
- Full-Stack Engineer
- AI Engineer

**Mission**
Enable MSMEs and growth-stage startups to unlock real business results from AI and modern software—without needing in-house technical teams.

**Vision (5 years)**
Be a recognized boutique AI \& software studio in APAC that:

- Powers dozens of MSMEs with AI-augmented operations, and
- Serves as a trusted product/AI partner for global startups and SaaS companies.

***

## 2. Services \& Value Proposition

### 2.1 Core Services

**Productized Offers (for MSMEs)**

1. **AI Customer Support Stack (4–6 weeks)**
    - Modern responsive website/landing
    - AI chatbot integrated with website + Messenger/WhatsApp/Viber
    - FAQ knowledge base, lead capture, escalation to human
    - Basic analytics dashboard (volumes, response times, common questions)
2. **AI Workflow Automation for SMEs (4–8 weeks)**
    - Process mapping (2–3 key processes)
    - Automation (e.g., reminders, reports, simple approvals, summaries)
    - Internal dashboard (tasks, logs, simple KPIs)
    - Training \& handover
3. **Vertical Packages** (start with 1–2 verticals, e.g., clinics / training centers / restaurants)
    - Predefined workflows and UX tailored to that vertical
    - Integration with existing tools (e.g., booking, POS, spreadsheets)

**Custom Development (for global startups / larger SMEs)**

- End-to-end web/mobile product builds
- AI features: search, copilots, RAG workflows, internal tools
- Cloud architecture, DevOps, scalable designs

**Ongoing Services (for both segments)**

- App \& infra **maintenance** (patches, uptime, bug fixes)
- AI **model \& prompt maintenance** (drift, API updates, quality improvements)
- Minor feature enhancements under a retainer
- Analytics \& optimization reviews (monthly/quarterly)


### 2.2 Value Proposition

- **Business-first AI:** You sell increased revenue and reduced manual work—not “models” and buzzwords.
- **Senior, compact team:** Direct access to founders with deep UX, full-stack, and AI engineering experience.
- **Productized yet custom:** Fast delivery through re-usable components; tailored workflows per client.
- **From build to maintenance:** Long-term partnership via retainers, not one-off “build and disappear”.

## 3. Market Analysis

### 3.1 Target Segments

**Primary: Philippine MSMEs (local market)**

- 99.5% of businesses; majority still under-digitized and under-served by IT/AI consultants.[^1][^2]
- Pain points: manual operations, fragmented tools (social, GCash, spreadsheets), slow customer response, lack of insights.[^2][^3]

**Secondary: Global startups \& SMEs (remote clients)**

- Need high-quality engineering and AI at lower TCO than US/EU agencies. PH dev rates (20–40 USD/h) are 60–80% below US rates.[^4][^5][^6]
- Priorities: speed, quality, strong communication, time-zone overlap.


### 3.2 Problem Summary

- MSMEs: “I know I should digitalize and try AI, but I don’t know where to start, and I’m scared of costs and complexity.”
- Global startups: “We need a small, senior team to own an AI-heavy product area, but US/UK agencies are too expensive.”


### 3.3 Competitive Landscape

- Local freelancers / small dev shops (mostly non-AI, weak UX, little product thinking).
- Larger PH outsourcing firms (slower, higher minimum engagement, not tailored to MSMEs).
- Global AI automation agencies (rarely PH-specific, usually higher pricing, focus on US/EU clients).

**Your differentiation:**

- “Boutique AI \& product studio from PH” with vertical productized offers and a strong maintenance/optimization layer.

## 4. Business Model \& Pricing

### 4.1 Revenue Streams

1. **Productized Projects (MSMEs)**
    - AI customer support stack:
        - Local pricing: ~PHP 150k–300k (≈ USD 2.5k–5k), depending on scope.
    - AI workflow automation:
        - Local pricing: ~PHP 200k–400k (≈ USD 3.5k–7k).
2. **Custom Builds (global startups \& larger SMEs)**
    - Full-stack/AI projects:
        - Pricing benchmark: 20–40 USD/h equivalent in project form.[^5][^4]
        - Typical project: 15k–60k USD depending on complexity and duration.
3. **Retainers (maintenance \& optimization)**
    - **Local MSMEs (PHP):**
        - Essential: PHP 15k–25k / month (bug fixes, monitoring, 1–2 small changes).
        - Growth: PHP 30k–60k / month (more hours, AI tuning, monthly review).
    - **Global clients (USD):**
        - 500–2,000 USD / month depending on scope (in line with lower end of AI automation retainers).[^7][^8]

### 4.2 Pricing Principles

- Anchor pricing to business outcomes (hours saved, sales/leads increased).
- Make entry offers low-risk (clear scope, clear result in 4–8 weeks).
- Every project contract includes a **maintenance option** with auto-renewal, cancel-anytime but default “stay”.

## 5. Go-To-Market Strategy

### 5.1 Acquisition (MSMEs)

- **Direct outreach + partnerships:**
    - Speak at local business events, chambers of commerce, industry associations.
    - Partner with accounting firms, marketing agencies, and BPOs as the “AI/tech partner” they refer.
- **Content \& credibility:**
    - Publish simple case studies: “How a clinic cut manual follow-up by 60% using an AI assistant.”
    - Short, Taglish-friendly content explaining practical AI benefits to business owners, not techies.


### 5.2 Acquisition (Global Startups/SMEs)

- **Portfolio \& outbound:**
    - Strong GitHub + portfolio of AI-heavy projects and case studies.
    - Targeted cold outreach to founders/CTOs; leverage your LinkedIn and existing network.
- **White-label / subcontracting:**
    - Build relationships with agencies or larger PH firms who lack deep AI capability.


### 5.3 Sales Process

1. Qualify: Identify whether they fit a **productized offer** or need a custom scope.
2. Discovery call: Understand 2–3 key pains and define success metrics.
3. Proposal: Choose productized package or custom SOW; include **retainer options** from day one.
4. Close: Start with a fixed-scope pilot; push for at least 3 months of post-launch support.

## 6. Operations Plan

### 6.1 Roles

- **UX Lead**
    - Discovery workshops, process mapping, UX/UI, prototypes, content direction.
- **Full-Stack Engineer**
    - Core app development, integrations, infra, performance, security.
- **AI Engineer**
    - AI workflow design, model selection, evals, prompt chains, observability, productionization.


### 6.2 Use of AI Internally

- Use AI coding assistants for: boilerplate, tests, refactors, code reviews.
- Use AI for documentation: initial drafts of tech docs, user guides, API docs.
- Use AI for internal ops: automated meeting notes, task extraction, client email drafting.

Humans retain control over:

- Architecture, critical security decisions, library/LLM selection.
- Complex AI engineering where documentation and APIs evolve rapidly.
- All client-facing strategy, diagnosis, and final code reviews.


### 6.3 Delivery Process

For productized offers:

1. **Discovery \& Mapping (week 1)**
2. **Blueprint \& UX (week 1–2)**
3. **Implementation (week 2–4/6)**
4. **Pilot (1–2 weeks)**
5. **Handover \& Training**
6. **Transition to Retainer** (pre-scheduled check-ins, performance reporting)

Project management: simple Kanban/sprint board, templated tasks per package.

### 6.4 Tooling

- Code: GitHub/GitLab, CI/CD (GitHub Actions, etc.).
- Infra: a default stack (e.g., FastAPI/Node + React/Next + managed Postgres + Vercel/Fly/Render).
- AI: OpenAI/Anthropic + retrieval (e.g., managed vector DB or Postgres pgvector), LangChain/LlamaIndex where appropriate.
- Ops: Notion/ClickUp/Linear for projects; Stripe/Xendit/PayMongo for billing; Google Workspace.

## 7. Growth \& Scaling Plan

### 7.1 Phase 1 (Year 1): Validation \& Base Revenue

Objectives:

- Close 5–10 MSME clients on productized packages.
- Land 1–3 global/startup clients for custom builds.
- Convert ≥ 50% of projects into maintenance retainers.

KPI targets:

- Monthly revenue target: enough to cover founder salaries + basic tools.
- Retainer AR (annualized recurring): aim for ≥ 20–30% of total revenue by end of year.


### 7.2 Phase 2 (Years 2–3): Systematization \& Delegation

Objectives:

- 10–20 active retainers covering a large portion of fixed costs (ideally 60–80%).
- Documented playbooks for each productized offer (checklists, code templates, deployment scripts).
- Hire 1–3 additional engineers or contractors for delivery.

KPI targets:

- Revenue: mix of 40–60% retained, 40–60% new projects.
- Client concentration: no single client > 25% of revenue.
- NPS and renewal rates: aim for 80%+ renewal on retainers.


### 7.3 Phase 3 (Years 3–5): Semi-Self-Running Operation

Objectives:

- Founders primarily handle: sales, architecture oversight, hardest AI work, partnerships.
- Day-to-day execution handled by team leads and standardized processes.
- Possibly introduce a light SaaS-style layer if a reusable internal tool emerges (e.g., reusable “AI support stack” admin portal).

KPI targets:

- Majority of net profit coming from retainers and existing clients.
- Founders’ operational time: target < 50% on delivery, decreasing over time.

## 8. Financial Plan (High-Level)

Assume: bootstrapped, small office/home office, 3 founders initially.

### 8.1 Costs (Year 1, rough)

- Founder compensation (modest, PH-based)
- Tools \& infra (dev, AI, PM, hosting): a few hundred USD/month.
- Marketing: small budget for events, ads, content creation.
- Legal/registration and accounting (PH context).

External benchmarks show PH senior developers at 3,500–5,200 USD/month in corporate settings; as founders you can decide lower initial cash comp plus profit share.[^5]

### 8.2 Revenue Scenarios (Year 1, simplified)

- 6–10 MSME projects at 2.5k–5k USD each
- 1–3 global projects at 10k–40k USD each
- 5–10 retainers at 300–1,000 USD/month blended

You can model conservative, base, and aggressive scenarios from this structure and adjust based on your target founder income and reinvestment plans.

## 9. Risk \& Mitigation

**Key Risks**

- MSMEs have limited budgets; some will churn or delay projects.
- Over-customization burns you out and weakens margins.
- AI ecosystem changes rapidly; vendor risk and tech debt.
- High dependence on a small set of clients early on.

**Mitigation**

- Strict productized scopes; no “infinite custom” unless priced accordingly.
- Modular architectures and vendor abstraction to swap LLMs/services.
- Diversify: local MSMEs + at least a couple of global startup clients + possibly one white-label partner.
- Strong onboarding and ROI reporting to increase retainer stickiness.
